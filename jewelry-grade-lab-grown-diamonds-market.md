<p><p>The jewelry-grade lab-grown diamond market primarily consists of two production methods: Chemical Vapor Deposition (CVD) and High-Pressure High-Temperature (HPHT). CVD diamonds are created by depositing carbon gases onto a substrate, allowing precise control over quality and characteristics. In contrast, HPHT diamonds mimic natural diamond formation by applying high pressure and temperature to carbon sources. Both methods yield high-quality, ethically sourced diamonds, catering to growing consumer demand for sustainable and conflict-free options in the jewelry sector.</p></p>
