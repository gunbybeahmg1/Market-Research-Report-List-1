<p><ul><li>Dopamine Antagonist</li><li>5-HT-3 Receptor Antagonist</li><li>NK1 Receptor Antagonist</li><li>Cannabinoid Receptor Antagonist</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The antiemetic drugs market is segmented into various types based on their mechanisms of action. Dopamine antagonists block dopamine receptors to reduce nausea and vomiting. 5-HT3 receptor antagonists inhibit serotonin receptors, effectively managing chemotherapy-induced nausea. NK1 receptor antagonists target neurokinin-1 receptors to control acute and delayed nausea. Cannabinoid receptor antagonists utilize the endocannabinoid system to alleviate symptoms associated with nausea. Each category plays a crucial role in treating diverse conditions, including cancer treatment and postoperative recovery.</p></p>
