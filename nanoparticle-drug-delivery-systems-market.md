<p><p>Nanoparticle drug delivery systems are advanced methods that enhance the efficacy and targeting of therapeutic agents. Liposomes are spherical vesicles made from phospholipid bilayers, used to encapsulate drugs for improved stability and bioavailability, enabling targeted delivery to specific tissues. Lipid nanoparticles, on the other hand, utilize solid or liquid lipids to create nanoparticles that protect and deliver mRNA vaccines and other therapeutics, offering advantages like better cellular uptake and reduced side effects. Both systems aim to optimize drug absorption and therapeutic outcomes.</p></p>
