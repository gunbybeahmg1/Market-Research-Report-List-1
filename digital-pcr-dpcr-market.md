<p><p>The digital PCR (dPCR) market comprises various types, primarily focusing on microfluidic dPCR and droplet digital PCR (ddPCR). Microfluidic dPCR utilizes microchannels to manipulate small volumes of liquid, enabling precise quantification of nucleic acids. In contrast, ddPCR encapsulates samples in droplets, allowing for partitioning and amplification, resulting in high sensitivity and accuracy. Both technologies enhance molecular analysis capabilities, driving growth in applications like oncology, genetic testing, and infectious disease detection, showcasing their unique advantages in the diagnostic landscape.</p></p>
